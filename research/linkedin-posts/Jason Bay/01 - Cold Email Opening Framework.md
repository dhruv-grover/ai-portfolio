## Core Idea

The purpose of the first line of a cold email is not simply to make the prospect read the second line.

Its job is to:

- Drive the email open
- Build urgency immediately

## Key Insights

- Buyers rarely read emails from top to bottom.
- Many scan in an F-pattern or only read the left side.
- The first line appears in preview text and is almost as important as the subject line.
- Use relevance instead of generic personalization.



### Effective Opening Types

- Trigger opener
- Tension opener
- Trust opener



## Practical Takeaways

- Lead with something happening now.
- Highlight a relevant business problem.
- Use social proof where appropriate.
- Make the first sentence impossible to ignore.
