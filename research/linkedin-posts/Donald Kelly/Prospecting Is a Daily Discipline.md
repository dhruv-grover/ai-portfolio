# Prospecting Is a Daily Discipline, Not a Spare-Time Activity

**Expert:** Donald Kelly  
**URL:** [LinkedIn Post URL](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/donaldckelly_i-get-it-the-lie-i-used-to-tell-myself-activity-7455565669884112896-bS0q?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADqypUsBkT9ygcGv9ZhtWV4kokXRCH2LsRo)

## Core Idea

The biggest obstacle to consistent prospecting is not a lack of time—it's prioritization. Salespeople often stay busy with administrative work while neglecting the activity that creates future pipeline.

---

## The "Too Busy" Trap

Donald reflects on believing he was too busy to prospect because his calendar was filled with proposals, meetings, research, and client work.

While these activities felt productive, they did little to generate new opportunities, creating a false sense of security until the existing pipeline began to decline.

---



## Measuring Where Time Goes

Tracking his schedule revealed that very little time was actually spent selling.

Referencing Salesforce research, Donald notes that the average salesperson spends only about **11 hours per week** in actual selling conversations.

This insight reinforced that being busy does not necessarily mean creating pipeline.

---



## Prospecting as a Daily Habit

Donald committed to protecting **two hours every day** exclusively for prospecting and pipeline generation.

Treating prospecting as a non-negotiable routine ensured that new opportunities were consistently entering the pipeline regardless of existing deal activity.

---



## Using AI to Increase Selling Time

Rather than replacing prospecting, AI should accelerate research by:

- Summarizing LinkedIn profiles.
- Gathering company insights.
- Identifying industry trends.
- Reviewing prospect content and interviews.

Reducing research time allows salespeople to spend more time starting meaningful conversations.

---



## Key Takeaways

- Pipeline is built through consistent daily prospecting.
- Busy work should never replace revenue-generating activities.
- Tracking time reveals whether effort aligns with selling priorities.
- AI should reduce research time, allowing more time for customer conversations.
- Focused, relevant outreach outperforms high-volume prospecting.
