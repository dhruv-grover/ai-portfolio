# Winning Before the Sales Conversation Begins

**Expert:** Donald Kelly  
**URL:** [LinkedIn Post URL](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/donaldckelly_sales-selling-activity-7450880409975943168-gD7s?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADqypUsBkT9ygcGv9ZhtWV4kokXRCH2LsRo)

## Core Idea

Buyers begin evaluating vendors long before they speak with sales. During this research phase, they develop evaluation criteria based on the content they consume, creating an informal "mental RFP" that influences purchasing decisions.

---

## The Mental RFP

Donald compares modern buying behavior to traditional software RFPs.

Just as many RFPs were unintentionally designed around a preferred vendor's solution, today's buyers build expectations based on the articles, webinars, and industry content they encounter during independent research.

This means vendors are often evaluated before the first sales conversation ever takes place.

---



## The Dark Funnel

A significant portion of the buyer journey now happens outside a company's visibility.

Prospects research solutions, compare vendors, and discuss options internally without engaging sales.

If your expertise is absent during this stage, competitors shape the buyer's understanding of:

- Important product capabilities.
- Evaluation criteria.
- Industry challenges.
- Success benchmarks.

---



## Content as the First Sales Call

Donald argues that educational content has become the earliest touchpoint in the sales process.

Rather than focusing only on outreach, sellers should consistently share:

- Industry trends and disruptions.
- Regulatory or market changes.
- Customer challenges.
- Lessons learned from real-world selling.

This positions the seller as a trusted source before direct engagement begins.

---



## Key Takeaways

- Buyers form opinions before speaking with sales.
- Educational content influences how prospects evaluate vendors.
- Consistent thought leadership increases visibility during the dark funnel.
- Strong positioning creates a competitive advantage before outreach begins.
- In modern B2B sales, content contributes directly to pipeline generation.
