# Cold Email Success Starts Before You Write the Email

**Expert:** Alex Berman

## Core Idea

Alex Berman argues that the success of cold email campaigns has very little to do with clever subject lines or perfectly crafted copy. Instead, consistently generating replies depends on two foundational elements: targeting the right prospects and ensuring emails actually reach their inboxes.

---



## Targeting Matters More Than Copy

Alex explains that many cold email campaigns fail before the first email is even sent because the prospect list is poorly built. In one example, a client targeted companies based on overall employee count rather than the size of their sales organization, causing the same messaging to be sent to vastly different audiences.

Rather than rewriting the email, Alex refined the LinkedIn Sales Navigator filters to better match the client's Ideal Customer Profile (ICP). This simple adjustment significantly improved response rates, demonstrating that audience selection often has a greater impact than email copy itself.

---



## Deliverability Is a Competitive Advantage

Modern cold email requires more than simply sending messages from a Gmail or Outlook account. Alex emphasizes that successful outbound campaigns rely on a properly configured email infrastructure that maximizes inbox placement.

His recommendations include using dedicated email-sending systems with custom SMTP, distributing email volume across multiple domains, and maintaining low sending volumes per inbox. These practices help preserve sender reputation while avoiding spam filters, allowing outreach campaigns to scale without sacrificing deliverability.

---



## Building a Scalable Cold Email System

Rather than chasing small improvements in subject lines or wording, Alex encourages outbound teams to optimize the underlying system. Accurate prospect data, verified contact information, and reliable email infrastructure create a foundation that consistently generates replies.

By treating targeting and deliverability as strategic priorities, companies can build outbound campaigns that produce predictable pipeline instead of relying on copywriting alone to improve performance.

---



## Key Takeaways

- Cold email performance depends more on targeting than creative copywriting.
- Well-defined prospect lists significantly improve response rates.
- Modern outbound requires strong email deliverability and infrastructure.
- Low-volume, high-quality outreach is more effective than high-volume mass emailing.
- Scalable cold email systems are built on accurate targeting and reliable inbox placement rather than subject line optimization.
