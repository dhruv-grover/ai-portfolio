# Recap and Roll Framework for Multi-Threaded Sales

## Core Idea

When selling to multiple stakeholders, don't restart discovery with every executive, and don't assume your champion has communicated everything perfectly.

Instead, use the **"Recap and Roll"** framework.

---

## The Two Common Mistakes

### 1. Restarting Discovery

Many sales reps begin every executive meeting by asking discovery questions from scratch.

This:

- Wastes the executive's time.
- Frustrates stakeholders.
- Makes the sales process feel repetitive.

---



### 2. Skipping Discovery Completely

Others assume the champion has already communicated everything.

This leads to:

- Hidden objections.
- Missing business priorities.
- Misalignment between stakeholders.

---



## The Recap and Roll Framework

Instead of restarting or skipping discovery:

### RECAP

Begin by summarizing everything learned during previous discovery conversations.

Share:

- Current business situation
- Problems identified
- Goals
- Success criteria

This demonstrates preparation and alignment.

---



### ROLL

Invite the new stakeholder to expand or correct the information.

Ask questions such as:

> "Here are the four things we've learned from your team. What should we add, subtract, or double underline?"

This allows executives to:

- Validate existing information.
- Add their own perspective.
- Reveal new priorities.
- Surface hidden concerns.

---



## Benefits

Using Recap and Roll allows you to:

- Avoid repeating discovery from scratch.
- Respect executive time.
- Build alignment across multiple stakeholders.
- Continuously improve your understanding of the buying committee.

---



## Key Takeaways

- Never restart discovery with every executive.
- Never assume your champion represents everyone's perspective.
- Recap previous learnings before every stakeholder conversation.
- Let each stakeholder "roll" the conversation forward by adding their own insights.
- Multi-threaded sales becomes faster, more collaborative, and more accurate when discovery evolves instead of restarting.
