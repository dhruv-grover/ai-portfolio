## Core Idea

The negotiation call is one of the highest-impact meetings in the sales cycle. Most revenue is lost through avoidable negotiation mistakes rather than poor pricing.

---

## The Five Biggest Negotiation Mistakes

### 1. Filling the Silence After Giving the Price

After stating your price:

- Explain pricing beforehand
- Give the number
- Stay silent
- Ask for their reaction

Avoid talking yourself into discounts.

---



### 2. Pitching When Price Objections Arise

When prospects say:

> "The price is too high."

Don't immediately justify your pricing.

Instead:

- Understand why they feel it's expensive.
- Diagnose the concern before responding.

---



### 3. Giving Discounts for Free

Never reduce price without receiving value in return.

Possible trade-offs include:

- More seats/licenses
- Longer contract commitment
- Earlier signature
- Faster payment

Every concession should have an equal exchange.

---



### 4. Negotiating Terms Too Easily

Avoid immediately offering:

- Split payments
- Better billing terms
- Free implementation

Small concessions accumulate and reduce overall deal value.

Always negotiate trade-offs instead of giving them away.

---



### 5. Artificially Anchoring High

Never inflate pricing just to create room for discounts.

If prospects discover inflated pricing:

- Trust is lost.
- Credibility decreases.
- Negotiations become harder.

Price honestly and negotiate transparently.

---



## Four Pricing Levers

Instead of negotiating only on price, Armand recommends using four variables that determine commercial terms.

### Pricing depends on:

1. How much the customer buys
2. How quickly they pay
3. Length of commitment
4. Forecast certainty (commitment to close)

These variables provide flexibility without simply lowering price.

---



## Negotiation Philosophy

Put every negotiation variable on the table upfront.

If customers ask for better pricing:

- Explain exactly what they receive.
- Explain exactly what you need in return.

This removes uncertainty and creates fair negotiations for both sides.

---



## Key Takeaways

- Stay silent after presenting pricing.
- Diagnose objections before defending your price.
- Never give discounts without receiving something valuable.
- Protect deal value by negotiating across multiple variables, not just price.
- Build transparent pricing frameworks that reduce negotiation anxiety for both buyer and seller.
