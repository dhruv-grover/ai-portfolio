## Core Idea

A successful enterprise sales process follows a structured progression:

- Cold Call
- Discovery
- Multithreading

Each stage has repeatable frameworks that improve qualification and deal progression.

---

## Cold Calling Framework

### 1. Heard the Name Tossed Around Opener

Instead of a generic introduction, use familiarity.

**Example**

> "Hey Jane, not sure if you knew but your team's full of 30MPC super fans. It's Armand from 30MPC, heard our name tossed around?"

Purpose:

- Reduce resistance
- Sound natural
- Establish credibility immediately

---

### 2. Problem Proposition

Lead with a problem rather than your product.

Example:

- Onboarding every rep
- Product training
- Discovery quality
- Multithreading

Offer help solving those business problems instead of pitching software.

---

## Discovery Framework

### 1. Situation Question

Start with an observation instead of asking generic background questions.

Example:

> "Your team downloaded our discovery cheat sheet and you're moving upmarket. Usually that means transitioning beyond surface-level discovery. Where am I off?"

This encourages prospects to correct or confirm your assumptions.

---

### 2. Operational Problem Question

Help prospects diagnose the issue.

Examples:

- Is discovery lacking depth?
- Or is it lacking breadth?

Rather than asking broad questions, narrow the choices to make conversations easier.

---

### 3. Business Impact Question

Connect operational problems to business outcomes.

Example:

> "When did you realize this became a problem?"

Follow-ups:

- Lost deals
- Sales cycle delays
- Missed revenue opportunities

---

## Multithreading Framework

### 1. Get Your Champion's Help

Ask directly for support.

Example:

> "Will you help me get Jane (VP Sales) and the other directors involved?"

The objective is to build a broader buying committee.

---

### 2. Champion Sandwich

Meet with your champion before and after stakeholder meetings.

Purpose:

- Prepare messaging beforehand
- Debrief afterwards
- Maintain alignment

---

### 3. Recap and Roll

Every new stakeholder should receive:

- A recap of existing discoveries
- An opportunity to add their own perspective

This builds discovery instead of restarting it for every conversation.

## Key Takeaways

- Open cold calls with familiarity and credibility.
- Lead conversations with business problems, not product features.
- Use structured discovery questions that uncover operational and business impact.
- Build relationships beyond a single champion through multithreading.
- Standardize every stage into repeatable playbooks for consistent execution.
