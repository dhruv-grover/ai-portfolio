# Personalized Outbound Email Framework

**Expert:** Armand Farrokh  
**URL:** [LinkedIn Post URL](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/armand-farrokh_you-could-write-ai-slop-recruiting-emails-activity-7445130358557372417-EehF/?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_android&rcm=ACoAADqypUsBkT9ygcGv9ZhtWV4kokXRCH2LsRo)

## Core Idea

The best outbound emails don't rely on AI-generated templates or generic personalization.

Instead, write one highly relevant sentence that proves you understand why this specific person is worth contacting.

---

## Example Email

**Subject:** 257% to President's Club

Instead of a generic recruiting email:

- Reference a specific accomplishment.
- Explain exactly why they caught your attention.
- Present a unique opportunity.
- End with a simple call-to-action.

Example:

> "Jane! This is probably a long shot given you're coming off a 257% year at Acme, but we're looking for a Founding AE at 30 Minutes to President's Club (and you're quite literally the perfect fit)."

The email continues by highlighting:

- Why the opportunity is unique.
- Why the recipient specifically fits the role.
- A low-friction CTA:

> "Open to hearing more?"

---



## Why It Works

The email immediately answers:

- Why this person?
- Why now?
- Why this opportunity?

Rather than relying on generic flattery or AI-generated copy.

---



## The Main Principle

Armand's rule is simple:

> If you can't write **one sentence** explaining why you want someone on your team, you shouldn't reach out to them at all.

The same principle applies to outbound prospecting.

Every outreach message should contain one highly specific observation that demonstrates genuine research.

---



## Key Takeaways

- Avoid generic AI-generated outreach.
- Personalize around a real achievement, signal, or milestone.
- Explain why the recipient was specifically chosen.
- Focus on relevance before persuasion.
- A single personalized sentence is often more valuable than an entire template.
