# Curiosity-Driven Prospect Research

**Expert:** John Barrows  
**URL:** [LinkedIn Post URL](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/johnbarrows_makeithappen-activity-7475176086696513536-AO39?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADqypUsBkT9ygcGv9ZhtWV4kokXRCH2LsRo)

## Core Idea

Great salespeople aren't simply taught to ask better questions—they become genuinely curious about the people they sell to.

Curiosity leads to better research, more authentic messaging, stronger discovery conversations, and ultimately better sales outcomes.

Rather than forcing curiosity, sales leaders should create conditions that naturally develop it.

---



## Curiosity Begins with Interest

John argues that curiosity doesn't appear out of nowhere.

Instead:

> Interest is the on-ramp to curiosity.

When something genuinely interests you, you're naturally motivated to learn more.

This leads to:

- Better account research
- More thoughtful outreach
- Better discovery questions
- More meaningful customer conversations

---



## Curiosity Improves Prospecting

Before prospecting into target accounts, John asks reps to complete one exercise.

Rather than simply qualifying an account, they must answer:

- Why do I personally want to work with this company?
- Do I admire their leadership?
- Do their values align with mine?
- Is this a product or service I'd genuinely enjoy selling?

Creating a personal connection encourages deeper research and more authentic outreach.

---



## Curious Reps Notice What Others Miss

During customer conversations, curious sellers don't only listen for direct answers.

They also pay attention to:

- Side comments
- Throwaway remarks
- Body language
- Hidden problems
- Unspoken opportunities

These small observations often reveal the biggest opportunities.

---



## AI Should Accelerate Curiosity, Not Replace It

John discusses how AI has changed research.

Search engines forced people to explore information, naturally creating curiosity.

AI can instantly provide answers, skipping much of that exploration.

Rather than replacing thinking, AI should help curious sellers research faster and dive deeper into topics that genuinely interest them.

The goal isn't faster answers.

The goal is better questions.

---



## Building Better Sales Teams

Sales leaders shouldn't simply tell reps:

> "Be more curious."

Instead they should build environments that encourage curiosity by:

- Helping reps connect personally with target accounts.
- Encouraging deeper account research.
- Rewarding thoughtful discovery.
- Using AI to accelerate learning rather than replace it.

Curiosity becomes a habit when people care about what they're learning.

---



## Key Takeaways

- Interest naturally develops curiosity.
- Curious sellers conduct better research and ask better questions.
- Personal connection to target accounts leads to more authentic outreach.
- Pay attention to subtle customer signals, not just direct answers.
- Use AI to deepen research, not eliminate the learning process.
- The best sales conversations begin with genuine curiosity, not scripted questions.
