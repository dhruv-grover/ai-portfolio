# Recovering Burned Accounts Framework

**Expert:** Morgan J. Ingram  
**URL:** [LinkedIn Post URL](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/morganjingramamp_consensuspartner-activity-7453067467092021248-MxzR/?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_android&rcm=ACoAADqypUsBkT9ygcGv9ZhtWV4kokXRCH2LsRo)

## Core Idea

If your territory has been over-contacted by previous sales reps, don't keep repeating the same outreach.

Instead of trying to repair the exact relationships that were damaged, create new paths into the account and rebuild trust from scratch.

---

## The Problem

Morgan describes a territory where prospects had been contacted repeatedly for years through generic "spray and pray" outbound.

Common response:

> "You guys have been calling me for six years. I don't want to talk to you."

Rather than fighting that perception, accept it and change your approach.

---



## Play 1: Find New Stakeholders

Avoid repeatedly contacting the same people.

Instead, identify:

- Newly hired executives
- Recently promoted managers
- New department leaders
- Fresh decision-makers who haven't experienced previous outreach

Examples include:

- New VP or Director hires
- Recently promoted Sales Enablement leaders
- New RevOps stakeholders

Find fresh relationships instead of reopening damaged ones.

---



## Play 2: Borrow Trust Through Internal Connections

Use LinkedIn Sales Navigator's **Connections Of** filter.

Look for:

- Existing customers
- Internal executives
- Board members
- Mutual connections

Warm introductions dramatically outperform another cold outbound attempt.

Instead of saying:

> "Can we schedule a meeting?"

Aim for:

> "Sarah suggested we connect."

Borrowing trust reduces resistance.

---



## Play 3: Enable Before You Sell

Morgan recommends leading with value rather than another pitch.

Examples include:

- Interactive product demos
- Educational resources
- Helpful content
- No-pressure insights

Importantly:

**No CTA.**

Give value first.

Knock second.

---



## Why This Works

Educational content succeeds because:

- It comes from a voice that isn't yours.
- Prospects can consume it on their own schedule.
- They begin building an internal business case without sales pressure.

This shifts conversations from skepticism toward curiosity.

---



## Rebuilding Account Reputation

Morgan emphasizes:

> Your reputation inside the account may not be your fault.

But rebuilding trust **is** your responsibility.

Recovering a burned territory requires patience, value creation, and entirely new relationship paths.

---



## Key Takeaways

- Stop repeatedly contacting stakeholders who have already rejected previous outreach.
- Target newly hired or newly promoted decision-makers.
- Use warm introductions whenever possible.
- Lead with educational value instead of sales pitches.
- Help prospects before asking for meetings.
- Rebuild trust through consistent, low-pressure engagement.
