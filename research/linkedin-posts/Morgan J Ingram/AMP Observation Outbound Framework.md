## Core Idea

Prospects rarely ignore **people**—they ignore **poor outreach**.

After analyzing 127 failed LinkedIn outreach attempts, Morgan found that the biggest problem wasn't personalization itself, but the lack of meaningful observations that demonstrate understanding of the prospect's business.

---



## The AMP Observation Outbound Formula

Every successful outbound message should follow this structure:

### 1. Observation

Show the prospect that you've actually researched them.

Reference a specific event such as:

- Product launch
- New executive hire
- Company expansion
- Recent announcement
- Hiring activity

Avoid generic introductions or compliments.

---



### 2. Context

Explain why your observation matters.

Help the prospect understand the business significance behind the event instead of simply mentioning it.

---



### 3. Pain Point

Connect the observation to a likely challenge the prospect is experiencing.

For example:

- New products require new messaging.
- Rapid hiring creates onboarding challenges.
- Expansion increases pipeline pressure.

The goal is to demonstrate that you understand their current situation.

---



### 4. Value Proposition

Explain how your solution helps solve the problem you've identified.

Focus on outcomes instead of features.

---



### 5. Call to Action

End with a simple, low-friction next step.

Make it easy for the prospect to continue the conversation.

---



## Before vs. After



### Poor Outreach

> "Hope all is well... My name is Morgan. We have synergies and I'd love to connect."

This message provides no reason for the prospect to respond.

---



### Better Outreach

Instead, reference a recent business event, explain why it matters, connect it to a likely business challenge, and ask a thoughtful question.

For example:

> "Saw your recent product launch and noticed you're hiring several AEs and SDRs. Curious how you're thinking about building messaging for the new product while scaling pipeline generation?"

The message demonstrates research, business understanding, and relevance.

---



## Why It Works

Successful outbound messaging helps prospects feel understood before attempting to sell.

As Morgan summarizes:

> "Show them you KNOW them before you show them what you DO."

Observation-based outreach creates more conversations because it connects business signals to meaningful business problems.

---



## Key Takeaways

- Poor outreach fails because it lacks relevant observations.
- Start every outbound message with a specific business signal.
- Connect observations to likely business challenges.
- Explain how your solution helps solve that challenge.
- End with a simple call to action.
- Prospects respond to relevance, not generic personalization.
