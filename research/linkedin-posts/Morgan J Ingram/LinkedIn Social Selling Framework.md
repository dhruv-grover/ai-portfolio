# LinkedIn Social Selling Framework

**Expert:** Morgan J. Ingram

## Core Idea

Successful social selling is not about sending more connection requests or more DMs.

It's a structured process that gradually builds familiarity, trust, and conversations before asking for a meeting.

Morgan shares the five-step framework one SDR used to book **17 meetings in one month**.

---



## Step 1: Identify High-Probability Prospects

Before sending any outreach, identify prospects with strong buying signals.

Look for:

- Companies your competitors actively sell to
- Newly hired executives (around 4 months into the role)
- Previous champions or closed-lost evaluators
- People connected to meaningful business events

The objective is to gather a unique observation before every interaction.

---



## Step 2: Engage Before Connecting

Build familiarity before requesting a connection.

If prospects post regularly:

- Leave thoughtful comments (1–2 sentences)
- Add meaningful insights—not generic praise

If they rarely post:

- Comment on posts they engage with
- Reply to their comments on other creators' posts
- Visit their profile and follow them

The goal is to become recognizable before sending outreach.

---



## Step 3: Connect

Morgan recommends sending a **blank connection request** for higher acceptance rates.

If the request isn't accepted:

- Withdraw the request
- Wait approximately three weeks
- Send another request later
- Continue engaging through comments and content

The objective is to maximize accepted connections within your Ideal Customer Profile (ICP).

---



## Step 4: Start Conversations

Don't immediately pitch after connecting.

Instead:

- Find a relevant observation
- Personalize the opening message
- Send a short video or voice note when appropriate
- Ask a thoughtful question based on something happening inside their business

Example topics include:

- Recent hires
- Product launches
- Team growth
- Industry trends

The conversation should feel natural rather than scripted.

---



## Step 5: Move to a Call

Once a genuine pain point emerges, transition the conversation off LinkedIn.

Morgan suggests:

- Sharing your booking link
- Offering three available meeting times
- Asking for their preferred calendar availability

Keep scheduling simple and frictionless.

---



## Why This Framework Works

Each step increases trust before making an ask.

Instead of treating LinkedIn as a cold outreach channel, the framework develops relationships through consistent engagement before requesting meetings.

This lowers resistance and creates warmer sales conversations.

---



## Key Takeaways

- Research prospects before sending outreach.
- Build familiarity through genuine engagement.
- Optimize connection acceptance before pitching.
- Personalize every conversation with relevant observations.
- Move conversations to calls only after identifying a real business problem.
- Social selling succeeds by building trust before asking for meetings.
