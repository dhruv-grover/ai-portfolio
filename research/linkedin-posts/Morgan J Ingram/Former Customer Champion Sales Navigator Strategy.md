## Core Idea

One of the most overlooked outbound opportunities is targeting **former customer champions** who have moved to new companies.

These buyers already understand your product, trust its value, and may now have fresh problems that your solution can solve again.

---



## Why This Strategy Works

When a customer champion changes companies, they bring:

- Existing product knowledge
- Previous positive experience
- Credibility within their new organization
- Awareness of the business problems your product solves

Instead of selling from scratch, you're restarting a relationship with someone who already knows your value.

---



## Sales Navigator Framework



### Step 1. Open Lead Filters

Navigate to:

> Sales Navigator → Lead Filters

---



### Step 2. Filter by Past Company

Under **Past Company**, add companies where you currently have customers.

This identifies people who previously worked at organizations already using your solution.

---



### Step 3. Filter by Current Company

Under **Current Company**, add:

- Target accounts
- Strategic prospects
- Companies from your ABM list

This narrows the search to customer champions who have joined accounts you want to penetrate.

---



### Step 4. Filter by Tenure

Add the filter:

> Less than 1 year in current role

This ensures the prospect has had enough time to understand their new company's challenges while still being early enough to influence purchasing decisions.

---



## Ideal Prospect Profile

The highest-value prospects are people who:

- Used your product successfully in a previous role.
- Recently joined one of your target accounts.
- Are beginning to evaluate solutions for similar business problems.

These contacts are significantly warmer than cold prospects because trust has already been established.

---



## Why Sales Navigator Matters

Morgan emphasizes that Sales Navigator is one of the most underutilized outbound tools.

By saving this search, sales teams can continuously identify former customer champions entering target accounts without manually researching every company.

---



## Results

According to Morgan, one SDR applied this strategy after learning it through free content and was promoted from a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) to an Account Executive role within three months.

The framework creates a repeatable source of warm outbound opportunities and qualified pipeline.

---



## Key Takeaways

- Former customer champions are one of the highest-converting outbound audiences.
- Use **Past Company** and **Current Company** filters together in Sales Navigator.
- Focus on prospects who have been in their current role for less than one year.
- Build saved searches to surface new opportunities automatically.
- Prioritize warm relationships before investing in completely cold outbound.
