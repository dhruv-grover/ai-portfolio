## Task 2: Research Project



### Topic

**Cold Outreach Pipeline for B2B SaaS**

### Objective

The objective of this research project was to identify leading practitioners in B2B SaaS cold outreach and build a structured knowledge repository by collecting their recent LinkedIn content, YouTube transcripts, and supporting resources. The goal was not simply to archive content, but to organize actionable insights that can later be transformed into a practical outbound playbook.

### What the Repository Contains

- A curated list of industry experts with source links and personalized research annotations.
- LinkedIn posts converted into structured markdown summaries for easy reference and comparison.
- YouTube transcripts collected from each expert's most relevant recent videos.
- Python scripts for automated YouTube metadata collection, transcript retrieval, and relevance ranking.



### Experts Researched

The project includes research from practitioners actively building and teaching modern outbound systems. Each expert was selected for a specific area of expertise within the cold outreach pipeline.

- **Jason Bay** – Selected for his expertise in cold calling, outbound messaging, and sales prospecting frameworks.
- **Morgan J. Ingram** – Chosen for his insights into enterprise outbound sales, SDR development, and multichannel prospecting.
- **Armand Farrokh** – Selected for his practical frameworks on sales execution, discovery, qualification, and pipeline management.
- **John Barrows** – Chosen for his extensive experience in B2B sales training, prospecting, objection handling, and sales methodologies.
- **Donald Kelly** – Selected for his content on consistent prospecting habits, outbound sales fundamentals, and pipeline generation.
- **Connor Murray** – Chosen for his modern outbound strategies, AI-assisted prospecting, and scalable cold outreach systems.
- **Michael Humblet** – Selected for his thought leadership on credibility, buyer trust, and relationship-driven B2B sales.
- **TK Kader** – Chosen for his founder-led perspective on GTM strategy, demand generation, and predictable pipeline growth for SaaS companies.
- **Taylor Haren** – Selected for his expertise in cold email, LinkedIn outreach, personalization, and outbound messaging.
- **Alex Berman** – Chosen for his deep expertise in cold email systems, deliverability, outbound automation, and scalable lead generation.



### What the Scripts Do

The Python scripts automate the collection and organization of YouTube research data.

They are responsible for:

- Fetching videos from an expert's YouTube channel within a specified date range.
- Ranking videos based on relevance to the research topic using predefined outbound sales keywords.
- Selecting the highest-value videos for transcript collection.
- Retrieving transcripts using the Supadata API, with the YouTube Transcript API available as a fallback.
- Saving transcripts and metadata into a consistent folder structure for further analysis.



### Possible Further Steps

The collected repository is designed to serve as a structured knowledge base rather than a final deliverable. A natural next step would be to use a Large Language Model (LLM) to analyze the accumulated LinkedIn posts and YouTube transcripts, identify recurring themes, compare strategies across experts, and synthesize the findings into a comprehensive **Cold Outreach Pipeline for B2B SaaS**.

The accumulated research can be used to generate:

- A step-by-step outbound playbook derived from the combined expertise of all researchers.
- Best practices for prospecting, messaging, follow-up, objection handling, and pipeline generation.
- Common patterns and areas of agreement or disagreement between experts.
- Actionable frameworks and checklists for SDRs, Account Executives, and founders.
- A searchable knowledge base or AI assistant capable of answering questions using the collected research.
